CREATIVE TIME: NOURISHING MY INNER ARTIST

During my creative time, I focus solely on the act of creation—no deadlines, no judgments.
It’s a way to enjoy the freedom of simply making, whether I'm in a quiet corner with my tools or in a bustling environment where inspiration hits.
This ritual clears mental clutter, promotes problem-solving, and provides an emotional release, acting as a therapeutic outlet.

A "HOW TO GUIDE" ON HOW TO PRACTICE CREATIVE TIME

Here are some steps to unleash your creativity and find clarity:

1. Set a Dedicated Time:
Choose a specific time each day or week to dedicate to your creative practice. It could be as short as 15 minutes or as long as an hour, depending on your schedule. Consistency is key, so try to stick to it.
2. Create a Relaxing Environment:
Find a space that inspires you—whether it’s a quiet corner, a cozy nook, or even outdoors. Make sure it’s free from distractions so you can fully immerse yourself in the creative process.
3. Gather Your Tools:
Whether you’re drawing, painting, writing, or designing, gather the materials you need beforehand. This will help you dive straight into your creative flow without interruptions.
4. Focus on the Process, Not the Outcome:
Let go of any pressure to create something “perfect.” Creative time is about exploration, not the final product. Allow yourself to experiment and enjoy the process without any expectations.
5. Embrace Imperfection:
Give yourself permission to make mistakes and try new things. Creativity thrives in moments of trial and error. Celebrate the journey, not just the end result.
Disconnect from Distractions:
Put away your phone and any other distractions during this time. If you need music, choose something calming or inspiring. The goal is to be fully present in your creative space.
Reflect and Appreciate:
After your session, take a moment to reflect on how you feel. Did you enjoy the process? Did it help clear your mind or spark new ideas? This reflection helps reinforce the positive impact of creative time.
Make It a Habit:
Over time, make creative time a regular part of your routine. The more you practice, the easier it becomes to tap into your creativity and use it as a tool for emotional well-being and problem-solving.
